\section{Game Selection Reference}

Atari-100K games grouped by reward density. Determines how much
learning signal vanilla DQN receives within the 100K step budget.

\vspace{0.3em}
\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
\setlength{\tabcolsep}{12pt}
\begin{tabular}{l l}
\toprule
\textbf{Category} & \textbf{Games} \\
\midrule
Dense    & Road Runner, Crazy Climber, Kung Fu Master, Up N Down, \\
         & Krull, Demon Attack, Chopper Command \\[4pt]
Moderate & Seaquest, Frostbite, Hero, Alien, Asterix, Ms.\ Pac-Man, \\
         & BattleZone, Kangaroo, Breakout, Jamesbond, Boxing, Gopher \\[4pt]
Sparse   & Pong, Freeway, Private Eye, Bank Heist \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\noindent{\small \textbf{Table 1.} Dense: frequent scoring through basic
actions. Moderate: regular scoring but requires learned behavior.
Sparse: long stretches of zero reward. Published scores in Schwarzer et
al.\ (2021), Table 4.}
