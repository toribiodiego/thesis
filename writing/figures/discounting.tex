\caption{The discount rate determines which path an agent prefers.
  Left: a small grid-world with a nearby reward ($+1$, one step
  from $s_0$) and a distant reward ($+3$, four steps). Right: the
  same decision as a tree. With a low discount
  rate~(\subref{fig:discount-low}), the distant $+3$ is discounted
  to $0.375$, so the agent takes the short path. With a high
  discount rate~(\subref{fig:discount-high}), the distant reward
  retains most of its value, so the agent walks further.}
\label{fig:discounting}
\end{figure}
